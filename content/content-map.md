# YouOweMe Content Map

Updated: 2026-05-16

This is the working map for planning YouOweMe content across Shorts, TikTok, Instagram Reels, Instagram carousels, and 1-2 minute YouTube landscape videos.

The core category is:

**Real-life money between people.**

The content should not feel like "finance app content." It should feel like specific human situations people recognize immediately, with practical help before the product appears.

## Current Strategy Shift

The previous content direction was emotionally correct, but too much of it lived in the same pattern:

```text
Awkward money situation -> app solves it
```

That is true, but repeated too often it starts to feel like app advertising.

The new required pattern is:

```text
Specific relatable story -> useful insight/template -> app makes it easier
```

Every future content piece should give value before the viewer downloads the app.

## Non-Negotiable Creative Rules

Every new story, Short, carousel, Reel, TikTok, or landscape video must follow these rules:

1. Lead with a specific human situation, not the app idea.
2. Use exact relationships: Dad, Mom, sister, roommate, partner, friend, client.
3. Use exact amounts whenever possible: $38, $50, $100, $12, $48.
4. Use exact confusion: "what did that payment cover?", "was this for lunch or the ticket?", "which bill was repaid?"
5. Give useful value before the app appears.
6. Reveal the product after the viewer understands the pain.
7. Do not open with polished product graphics too often.
8. Vary the visual format so the feed does not feel repetitive.
9. Do not make family money feel like debt collection.
10. Do not make people look irresponsible, angry, or guilty.

## Best Current Positioning

YouOweMe is not just a debt tracker.

It is a calm record for real-life money between people.

Best emotional framing:

```text
Stop making one person remember everything.
```

Best practical framing:

```text
Track what was paid, what repeats, what was repaid, and what is still open.
```

Best CTA:

```text
Track it once. Reconcile calmly.
```

## How To Choose The Next Content Piece

Use this order:

1. Choose a content series.
2. Choose a real relationship.
3. Choose a specific money moment.
4. Add exact amounts or exact items.
5. Choose the confusion.
6. Choose the useful takeaway or template.
7. Add the product bridge late.
8. Choose the format.
9. Record results after posting.

New formula:

```text
Series + Relationship + Exact Amount/Item + Confusion + Useful Takeaway + Product Bridge + Format
```

Example:

```text
Family Accountant + Dad/Mom + $100 repayment + what did it cover + track each charge separately + YouOweMe history + Short
```

Becomes:

```text
Dad sent $100. But what did it cover?
```

## Production Constraints

- No face on camera.
- No personal voice recording.
- Shorts use ElevenLabs narration and Remotion.
- Shorts use word-level timing for captions and animation.
- Landscape videos use HyperFrames and Kokoro narration.
- Visuals can use generated images, exact amount breakdowns, message bubbles, receipts, phone-in-hand scenes, app screenshots, text animation, and simple product demos.
- Shorts should usually be 25-35 seconds.
- Landscape videos should usually be 60-120 seconds.
- Instagram carousels should be saveable and practical.

## Shorts / TikTok / Reels Rules

Goal:

Stop the scroll with a specific money situation and deliver one useful insight before the app appears.

Length:

- usually 25-35 seconds
- one idea only
- 45-75 spoken words is often better than 90+ if the hook is sharp

Required structure:

1. 0-2s: specific hook already in motion.
2. 2-10s: exact situation with relationship and amount.
3. 10-20s: complication or useful insight.
4. 20-28s: product bridge.
5. final line: simple CTA.

Best short CTA:

```text
Track it once. Reconcile calmly.
```

Other CTAs:

- "Keep the history clear with YouOweMe."
- "Clear, not awkward."
- "Keep a running balance."
- "Do not let it live in your head."

Do not:

- show the app in the first second every time
- start with "Managing money can be hard"
- explain too many features
- make it feel like a product tour unless it is a Track It Once demo

## Carousel Rules

Goal:

Earn saves and shares by giving a useful template, checklist, or exact breakdown.

Length:

- 6 slides by default
- 8 slides only when the topic needs more explanation

Required structure:

```text
Slide 2: Real situation
Slide 3: Why it gets awkward
Slide 4: Exact text/template/checklist
Slide 5: How to track it clearly
Slide 6: CTA to YouOweMe
```

Good structure:

```text
Specific situation
Small complication
Emotional truth
Product bridge
Simple CTA
```

Example structure:


Do:

- keep scripts specific
- use real situations
- use exact amounts
- create a small mystery or confusion
- make the first two seconds feel alive
- give the viewer a useful insight or phrase
- let the product appear after the pain is clear

Do not:

- make every video feel like "money is awkward, use YouOweMe"
- show the app too early in every video
- rely only on polished visuals
- make all videos visually identical
- use vague hooks like "managing money can be hard"
- use broad finance-app language before the story

## Decision Rules After 14 Days

Double down if:

- a hook gets much higher views than average
- people watch to the end
- people comment
- people share/save
- profile visits increase

Stop or reduce if:

- the format repeatedly gets low watch time
- the first two seconds do not hold viewers
- the topic feels too generic
- the post looks too much like an ad
- the product appears before the story creates interest

Keep testing:

- parents/caregiving
- partial repayments
- exact amount confusion
- awkward message templates
- family accountant mental load

Reduce:

- generic "awkward money" posts
- broad finance app messaging
- product-first posts
- repetitive app mockup videos with no specific story

## Creative Standards

Every piece should pass these checks:

- Does the first line name a specific situation?
- Does it include a real relationship?
- Does it include an exact amount, item, or repayment confusion?
- Would someone watch even if they did not know the app exists?
- Is there a useful takeaway, text, checklist, or insight before the CTA?
- Does the product appear as relief, not interruption?
- Is the visual format different enough from recent posts?
- Does it avoid generic finance language?
- Does it avoid debt collector energy?
- Can the same idea become a carousel, Short, or landscape video later?
