# YouOweMe Content Map

This file is the working map for planning YouOweMe content across Shorts, Instagram carousels, and 1-2 minute YouTube landscape videos.

The core content category is not "finance app tips." It is:

**Money between people.**

The app is the resolution. The story is the attention engine.

## How To Use This File

When creating new content, choose in this order:

1. Pick a story territory.
2. Pick an audience lane.
3. Pick a story pattern.
4. Pick a format.
5. Choose the product bridge and destination.

This keeps content varied while still pointing attention toward the app.

## Production Constraints

The content system must fit the current workflow:

- No face on camera.
- No personal voice recording.
- Shorts use scripted narration generated in ElevenLabs.
- Shorts are built in Remotion after word-level timing is produced from the narration.
- YouTube landscape videos are built in HyperFrames with Kokoro narration.
- Visuals can use generated images, app screenshots, UI mockups, text animation, kinetic typography, and simple story scenes.
- Shorts should usually be around 25-35 seconds.
- Landscape videos should usually be around 60-120 seconds.
- Instagram carousels should be readable as standalone educational/story posts.

The content should be efficient to produce. Prefer repeatable formats over one-off complicated ideas.

## Core Positioning

YouOweMe helps people keep money between people clear before it becomes awkward, unfair, or forgotten.

The product is useful because real-life money is rarely just math. It includes memory, timing, tone, fairness, recurring costs, partial repayments, and relationships.

## Story Patterns

These are the reusable engines. Each topic should use one of these patterns.

### Pattern A: The Tiny Scene

Structure:

1. Open with a recognizable scene.
2. Name the emotional tension.
3. Give a small useful rule.
4. Show how the app makes it easier.

Example:

"You paid for dinner three weeks ago. They said they would send it later. Now the hard part is not the $48. It is sounding normal when you ask again."

Best formats:

- Shorts
- carousels

### Pattern B: What To Say Instead

Structure:

1. Show the bad message.
2. Explain why it creates tension.
3. Replace it with a calmer message.
4. Connect to Money Conversations.

Best formats:

- Shorts
- carousels

### Pattern C: The Hidden Cost

Structure:

1. Start with something that looks small.
2. Reveal the hidden mental or relationship cost.
3. Give the better system.
4. Bridge to YouOweMe.

Best formats:

- Shorts
- landscape videos
- carousels

### Pattern D: Mistake / Better Move

Structure:

1. "The mistake is..."
2. "The better move is..."
3. Give a concrete example.
4. Show product fit.

Best formats:

- Shorts
- carousels

### Pattern E: Before / After

Structure:

1. Before: scattered notes, chat history, memory, receipts.
2. After: one balance, one history, one calm next step.
3. Product bridge.

Best formats:

- Shorts
- carousels
- landscape videos

### Pattern F: Psychology Explainer

Structure:

1. Name a surprising truth.
2. Explain the emotion.
3. Give a practical fix.
4. Connect to the app lightly.

Best formats:

- landscape videos
- carousels
- some Shorts

### Pattern G: Real-Life System

Structure:

1. Show a repeated situation.
2. Show why manual tracking fails.
3. Present a simple system.
4. Show app features as the system.

Best formats:

- landscape videos
- carousels

## Format Rules

### Shorts

Goal:

Stop the scroll with a human money moment, then deliver one useful emotional/practical insight.

Length:

- usually 25-35 seconds
- one idea only
- 75-95 spoken words is a useful target

Structure:

1. 0-3s: hook with tension
2. 3-10s: tiny story or scene
3. 10-22s: useful insight or script
4. 22-30s: product bridge
5. final line: soft CTA

Visual style:

- large text captions synced to narration
- generated story image or simple animated scene
- app screenshot appears after the viewer understands the problem
- use motion often enough to hold attention
- avoid long static text blocks

CTA examples:

- "Keep the balance clear in YouOweMe."
- "Use YouOweMe before the message gets awkward."
- "Track it now, ask calmly later."
- "Let the app remember the balance for you."

### Instagram Carousels

Goal:

Earn saves and shares by making the viewer feel understood and giving them a practical script or system.

Length:

- 6-10 slides
- one clear promise per carousel
- slide 1 must be a strong emotional hook

Structure:

1. Hook/title
2. The relatable situation
3. Why it feels hard
4. Mistake people make
5. Better rule/system
6. Example script or checklist
7. Product bridge
8. CTA

Visual style:

- text-first but not dense
- strong contrast
- one idea per slide
- use original designed graphics, app screenshots, or generated image backgrounds
- make the carousel original and clearly designed for the brand

CTA examples:

- "Save this before the next awkward money message."
- "Use this script next time."
- "Track the balance before you need the conversation."
- "Try YouOweMe for money between people."

### YouTube Landscape Videos

Goal:

Create a deeper, polished mini-guide that can live as searchable and shareable evergreen content.

Length:

- usually 60-120 seconds
- one central thesis
- can include 3-5 examples

Structure:

1. Open with a narrative problem.
2. State the thesis.
3. Explain 2-4 useful points.
4. Show a practical system or script.
5. Bridge to YouOweMe as the tool.
6. Close with calm, memorable line.

Visual style:

- HyperFrames animated scenes
- generated imagery
- app screenshots
- kinetic typography
- simple motion graphics
- do not rely only on narration

CTA examples:

- "YouOweMe is built for this kind of money: informal, repeated, and easy to forget."
- "When the money matters but the relationship matters too, keep the balance clear."

## Creative Standards

Every piece should pass these checks:

- Does the first line sound like a real human problem?
- Would someone send this to a friend, partner, sibling, or roommate?
- Is there one useful takeaway even if they do not download the app?
- Does the app appear as relief, not interruption?
- Is the story specific enough to feel real?
- Is the format fast enough for no-face/no-voice production?
- Can this be turned into another version later?

## Avoid

- Generic personal finance advice.
- Shame-heavy messaging.
- Debt collector energy.
- Overly polished corporate ad tone.
- Explaining too many app features at once.
- Making every post about "download the app."
- Abstract content that does not start from a real scene.
