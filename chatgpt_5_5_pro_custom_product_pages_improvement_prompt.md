## Important Updated Product Features

These features may not be fully reflected in the current custom product pages/screenshots. Please use them when deciding what screenshots and messages should be updated.

### Running Balance

Every entry updates one clear balance per person, so the user can see who owes whom without rebuilding history from chats, notes, bank transfers, or memory.

Good for:

- IOUs;
- family money;
- couples;
- roommates;
- shared expenses;
- clients;
- temporary support;
- long-running balances;
- partial repayments.

### Relationship Timeline

Timeline connects money movement with money communication. For each person, it can include:

- balance changes;
- repayments;
- separate loans;
- follow-ups;
- repayment updates;
- loan requests;
- shared statement links;
- PDF statements;
- text exports;
- CSV exports.

This matters because people often need context before sending another message.

### Loan Records

Loan Records keep one specific loan or repayment plan in its own clean space without creating another person or losing the main history.

Users can start a separate record for:

- car repair loan;
- rent deposit;
- family bill;
- client project;
- temporary support arrangement;
- larger or longer-running balance;
- structured repayment plan.

Loan Records can include:

- repayments;
- extra amounts;
- corrections;
- notes;
- due dates;
- percentage interest;
- fixed interest;
- images;
- own remaining balance;
- connection back to the full person balance.

This is important for:

- Money Owed;
- Temporary Financial Support;
- Long-Term Balances;
- Client Payment Records;
- Family Reimbursements;
- possibly Couples if one partner temporarily supports another.

### Group Paybacks

Group Paybacks organize one shared thing one person paid for, such as:

- group gift;
- tickets;
- dinner;
- deposit;
- booking;
- shared purchase;
- office/team gift;
- small roommate or family shared cost.

The key user question:

`I paid first. Who paid me back, who partly paid, and who still owes?`

Group Paybacks are not meant to be full trip accounting or a collaborative group ledger. They are best when one person paid first and wants a simple record of paybacks from each person.

They should connect to:

- each person's running balance;
- partial payments;
- reminders;
- clear repayment status;
- possibly Money Conversations.

This may be a major missing conversion angle.

### Split Entry / Shared Expense Splitting

Split Entry lets the user record one shared expense and split it equally or exactly/custom between participants. It can support:

- dinners;
- tickets;
- groceries;
- trips;
- rent;
- client costs;
- household costs;
- custom uneven shares.

For two or three participants, there is visual/manual split control; for larger groups or receipt-based splits, manual entry can give exact amount control.

### Recurring Entries

Recurring Entries turn one entry into a schedule. Users can choose rhythms from daily to yearly, keep it tied to the right weekday/date, optionally set an end, and have real entries created as repeats come due.

Good for:

- rent;
- utilities;
- subscriptions;
- allowances;
- repeated household costs;
- parent subscriptions;
- recurring family reimbursements;
- couple/roommate shared costs.

### Payment Reminders

Payment reminders track upcoming, overdue, and completed reminders for collecting money or repaying someone else.

Good for:

- promised repayment dates;
- upcoming paybacks;
- recurring family/roommate/household bills;
- client due dates;
- long-term balances;
- temporary support check-ins.

### Smart Money Check-Ins

Smart Money Check-Ins watch real balances, repayment progress, reminders, promised dates, and silence.

They suggest when communication starts to matter, explain why now, and guide the user into a ready-to-send message.

Good for:

- Money Owed;
- awkward follow-ups;
- repayment updates when the user owes someone;
- partial repayments;
- relationship-safe communication.

### Money Conversations

Money Conversations turn the real balance and history into ready-to-send messages for:

- Follow-Up;
- Ask for Loan;
- Repayment Update.

The user chooses tone and keeps control before sending.

Messages can include Live Link, and Timeline can record that the communication happened.

Good for:

- Money Owed;
- Temporary Financial Support;
- Couples;
- Family;
- partial repayments;
- borrower-side repayment updates.

### Live Link

Live Link creates an always-current browser link to the latest balance and transaction history. Only one person needs the app. The other person does not need to install anything or create an account.

Good for:

- family;
- partners;
- roommates;
- clients;
- long-running balances;
- temporary support;
- reducing repeated screenshots.

### PDF Statements / Formal Records

PDF statements create clean, shareable statements from selected entries when a more formal summary helps.

The current/improved PDF statement capability can include:

- selected entries;
- what was recorded;
- what was paid;
- what remains open;
- current balance;
- due date;
- payment details;
- optional logo/image branding;
- wording/tone style such as more professional or more casual;
- client-facing or family-facing summaries.

PDF statements are useful for:

- client balances;
- small business records;
- family reimbursements;
- parent/caregiving records;
- long-term balances;
- formal summaries;
- possibly temporary support records.

Important boundary:

PDF statements are not accounting, tax, invoicing, legal, or payment-processing software.

### Interest Tracking

There are two interest modes:

1. Percentage interest
   - amount grows over time;
   - user enters rate;
   - frequency can be daily, weekly, monthly, or yearly;
   - can optionally include start and end dates.

2. Fixed / flat interest
   - one exact amount added once;
   - no rate or date calculation.

Interest can be included in:

- balances;
- entry totals;
- Loan Records;
- statements;
- shared links;
- repayment context.

This is important for:

- Long-Term Balances;
- Client Payment Records;
- formal money owed;
- larger loans.

Need to be careful with tone and compliance. Do not make the app sound like a lender or legal finance product.

### Voice to Entry

Users can log money by voice, e.g. "I lent Alex 30 euros for groceries yesterday." The app turns this into a structured entry.

Likely useful as a default-page feature or broad convenience feature, but probably not the primary conversion hook for most Custom Product Pages unless a page is about frequent entries.

### Privacy / Convenience

The app supports:

- offline use;
- no mandatory sign-up;
- Face ID / Touch ID lock;
- sync across devices;
- multiple cloud profiles;
- CSV export;
- multi-currency support.

These may matter as secondary trust/support points, especially for family, clients, parent expenses, or sensitive money records.
